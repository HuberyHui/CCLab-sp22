Simultaneous Contrast illusion

Go back Placing two objects of the same color against a background of different contrast can make them look as if they have become different colors. In visual design, this phenomenon is known as the simultaneous contrast illusion, and the effect may be perceived differently by different people. Unfortunately, there is no solid theory as to why this illusion occurs, but there is plenty of research to speculate on why, and lateral inhibition may be one of the causes.
